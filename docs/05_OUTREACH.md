# 05_OUTREACH.md — Network Message & Follow-ups

> The network is the asset. These people already know your work. The message
> is a soft availability signal, not a hard sell. Keep it short and confident.

## Primary message (group channel, Day 7 AM)

---
Hey all — quick update. I finally put my own work in one place:
shubhbuilds.com

I'm opening up for website projects now. Three tiers — Standard ($200),
Pro ($500), Elite ($1k+, full 3D/motion). You've seen Breathify, Cortex,
and the furniture viewer; the portfolio pulls them together plus a client
demo.

If any of you ever have overflow website work you can't get to, keep me in
mind — fast turnaround, and I'll treat your referral's project like it's
my own reputation on the line (because it is).

Portfolio: shubhbuilds.com
---

## Why this works
- Leads with proof (the live link), not a request.
- Names the real builds they already respect.
- "overflow work you can't get to" matches exactly how the group already
  operates — you're slotting into an existing behavior, not asking a favor.
- The reputation line tells senior people you understand referrals are trust
  loans. That's the thing that makes them comfortable handing you a client.

## Individual DM (to anyone who reacts/replies)

---
Appreciate it. If something comes up that fits, I can usually turn a first
draft around in a few days and keep you in the loop the whole way. Want me to
send a couple of detail shots of the [Cortex graph / furniture viewer] so you
know the level I work at?
---

## If someone offers a real client — first reply
Keep it calm and competent. Ask the three things that de-risk the job:
1. "What's the business and who's it for?"
2. "Any site/brand assets already, or starting from zero?"
3. "What's the timeline and budget they're working with?"
Then: "Give me [X hours] and I'll send a direction + a rough first look."

## At the meetup (60-sec walkthrough)
1. Open shubhbuilds.com — let the loader + hero land (that's the hook).
2. Scroll to Work — talk 10 sec each on Cortex and the 3D viewer (the real
   engineering is what earns respect in that room).
3. Open SparkClean — "and here's what a client site looks like."
4. Close: "I've got bandwidth for one or two projects right now — if anyone's
   overloaded, send them my way."

## Hard rule
Do NOT fabricate past clients or testimonials to this group. They're senior
engineers; they'll spot it, and getting caught lying ends the referral
pipeline permanently. The honest pitch — "young operator, real shipped
builds, hungry, fast" — is genuinely strong here. Use it.
